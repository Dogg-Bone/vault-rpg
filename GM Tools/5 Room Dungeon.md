Dungeon ~ Adventure
Room ~ Scene
Generalized to the **5 Scene Adventure** 

1. Entrance / Inciting incident
2. Puzzle / Rising tension
3. Setback / Tension continues to rise 
4. Climax
5. Resolution / Falling tension


> [!tip] Shuffle rooms
> Example: surprise [[Vagabonds]] by swapping Resolution and Climax rooms

> [!tip] Theme
> A coat of paint. Choose a faction or a concept within a faction. The Root board game may have inspiration.

>[!tip] Resolution
>Reward or twist that serves as a hint or hook for the next adventure - foreshadow the [[Big Bad Evil Guy]] or personalize something to your vagabonds' history, drives, or connections

> [!tip] Exploration
> Place interactables in each scene. Describe them when the [[Vagabonds]] enter in a way that indicates they can be interacted with, that there is something to be gained by the interaction. These interactables could be NPCs or creatures or scrolls or 'strange' text or 'locked' boxes or 'suspicious' barrels of fish or scratches/breadcrumbs leading to a disguised exit
## Ideas for Interactables
### 📜 Documents & Texts

- **The Blank Pardon:** A wax-sealed scroll tube containing an official faction pardon, complete with a signature but left completely blank for the recipient's name.
    
- **The Smuggler's Ledger:** A waterlogged Riverfolk Company ledger detailing recent trade routes, with a supposedly "abandoned" clearing circled heavily in red ink.
    
- **Archaic Carvings:** Strange text carved deep into the heartwood of an ancient oak tree, which only reveals its meaning when traced with a vagabond's paw.
    
- **The Buried Manifesto:** A hastily concealed Woodland Alliance propaganda poster that has a crude, hand-drawn map of a nearby patrol route on the back.
    
- **The Torn Journal:** A worn vagabond’s diary detailing a secret path through the deep woods, with the crucial last three pages violently torn out.
    
- **The Intercepted Orders:** A discarded, violently shredded Eyrie Dynasty decree that hints at an impending attack on a nearby neutral clearing.
    

### 📦 Objects & Loot

- **The False-Bottomed Barrel:** A suspiciously heavy barrel of salted fish that sloshes awkwardly, revealing a hidden compartment beneath the catch.
    
- **The Hollow Hammer:** A rusted fox-iron crafting hammer with an unscrewable handle, currently hiding a small rolled-up schematic.
    
- **The Trapped Cheese:** A half-eaten wheel of sharp badger-cheese left on a stump, hiding a small, swallowed iron key inside.
    
- **The Locked Music Box:** A reinforced, locked box that lacks a keyhole and only springs open when a specific woodland folk melody is whistled nearby.
    
- **The Gunpowder Herbs:** A standard-looking satchel of dried medicinal herbs that smells dangerously of black powder.
    
- **The Surveyor's Tool:** An abandoned Marquise de Cat surveyor’s transit, its brass bent and lenses cracked, but still highly valuable to a tinker.
    

### 🌲 Environment & Scenery

- **The Disguised Exit:** Deep claw scratches on a wooden doorframe that point downward, hinting at a hidden trapdoor buried beneath a rug.
    
- **The Hot Ashes:** A seemingly abandoned campfire with no smoke, but the thick bed of ashes is still searing hot to the touch.
    
- **The Dead Drop:** A perfectly hollowed-out log marked with a subtle knife notch, currently containing a single, highly polished acorn.
    
- **The Untouched Tent:** A pristine, unlooted supply tent standing completely ignored in the middle of an otherwise ransacked camp.
    
- **The Vagabond Shrine:** A makeshift, mossy shrine to the forest spirits littered with small, shiny trinkets, silver coins, and a freshly lit candle.
    
- **The Aggressive Briars:** A thick patch of thorny briars that seems to have grown rapidly and unnaturally overnight to block a specific cave entrance.
    

### ⚙️ Mechanisms & Puzzles

- **The Sawmill Gear:** An exposed, rusted gear from an old Marquise sawmill that, if forced to turn, alters the water level of a nearby stream and reveals a flooded path.
    
- **The Owl Statues:** A set of three stone owl statues marking a crossroads; one is missing its carved wooden eyes, which are lying in the dirt nearby.
    
- **The Alarm Snare:** A tripwire made of nearly invisible spider-silk that doesn't trigger a weapon, but rather drops a net full of loudly jingling brass bells.
    
- **The Humming Arch:** A crumbling stone archway covered in vines that vibrates and hums with energy when a crafted weapon is drawn near it.
    
- **The Mechanical Bird:** A rusted cage hanging from a high branch, containing a clockwork bird that squawks a coded, repeating phrase when its key is wound.
    
- **The Weighted Pressure Plate:** A loose floorboard that requires exactly the weight of one fully equipped vagabond to keep a nearby portcullis open.
    

### 🐾 Flora, Fauna & NPCs

- **The Stubborn Courier:** A wounded, extremely stubborn messenger pigeon who refuses to hand over its message cylinder to anyone not wearing a specific faction uniform.
    
- **The Spore Cloud:** A patch of bioluminescent mushrooms that provides excellent light but will release a disorienting, hallucinogenic spore cloud if stepped on.
    
- **The Armored Hive:** A swarm of angry honeybees that have built their bustling hive inside the chest cavity of a discarded, hollowed-out suit of plate armor.
    
- **The Sleeping Mercenary:** A heavily-armored badger mercenary asleep at their post, clutching a finely crafted, very stealable broadsword.
    
- **The Mimic Squirrels:** A pair of chattering squirrels in the canopy who seem to be flawlessly mimicking the exact voices and current conversation of the party.
    
- **The Toll Turtle:** A massive, ancient snapping turtle resting perfectly in the center of a muddy thoroughfare, demanding a "toll" of fresh, high-quality food to move aside.
