# The Sunken Tomb
In the forest between [[Barewick Clearing]] and [[Drytown Clearing]] lies an old decrepit crypt, it's entrance nestled in a grove of birch trees. 
[[Map of the Sunken Tomb]]
The walls of the crypt are decorated with engravings that seem to tell the story of a Knight fighting against a mythical serpent.

### Room 1
Steps lead down into a flooded chamber of rough-hewn stones. Seven pillars rise to the ceiling, the eighth fallen crosswise along the center of the room, rising above the surface of the water. The stagnant water carries a sickly sweet scent of rotting fruit. A portion of the wall to your right has caved in. Vines hang down over an irregular opening behind the fallen wall. Against the intact wall stands a stone statue of a solemn-faced mouse resting its hands on the pommel of a long, rusty metal sword stood on its point. A fallen archway constricts the exit at the far end of the room.
A [[Giant Centipede]] lurks in the water behind the fallen pillar.

```statblock
creature: "Giant Centipede"
```
### Room 2
After passing through the cramped fallen archway, more stair lead to another flooded chamber. It's totally dark. The place smells like a humid greenhouse, and you can hear the quiet burbling of water coming from somewhere up above. When lit, it's clear the water is much deeper than in the previous room. Moss and vines cover the walls, including vines leading up to an opening overhead. A small trickle of water descends from the opening. Under the deep water sleeps a [[Sibilant Snake]]. 
```statblock
creature: Sibilant Snake
```
The snake is very dangerous, but can be reasoned with. It is trapped in this chamber, too large to leave. This snake is particularly fond of riddles. If the Vagabonds solve the snake's riddle, it will allow them passage through an underwater tunnel below the surface. Under the water, there are remnants of the snake's victims, including a ruby-encrusted golden bracelet on the wrist of a partial rabbit skeleton.
### Room 3
The vine-covered opening in the wall of Room 1 leads to a small cave above Room 2. Water trickles down into a three-foot wide hole that drops into Room 2. The tunnel is collapsed just beyond the hole, but the rubble is loose and can be moved.  If the Vagabonds attempt to dig, they roll with Finesse. On a hit, they mark 2 exhaustion each and may proceed to Room 4. On a partial hit, they both mark an additional exhaustion. On a miss, the tunnel collapses down into Room 2. This disorients the [[Sibilant Snake]], rendering it vulnerable, but also frees it to leave the ruin. 

### Room 4
A large, diamond shaped antechamber with heavy metal double doors shut against the far wall. A ray of sunlight streams in from the ceiling, illuminating dust particles in the air and falling directly on a moss-encrusted box in the center of the room, right in the middle of a [[Giant Centipede]] nest! The doors are locked and the key lies within the box. If the Vagabonds entered from Room 3, they emerge on a narrow stone ledge 15 feet off the floor, giving them the high ground and the element of surprise. If they entered through the underwater tunnel, they find themselves in a pool of water between the sleeping centipedes and the locked metal door. The nest consists of 4 four [[Giant Centipede]]s and one [[Giant King Centipede]].

```statblock
creature: Giant King Centipede
```

### Room 5
Beyond the double doors lies a dry, dust-choked circular chamber, miraculously untouched by the flooding that ruined the rest of the crypt. As the heavy doors groan open, stale air rushes in, disturbing a thick layer of dust. The room is dominated by a raised stone dais in the center. Atop the dais rests the pristine, armor-clad skeleton of an ancient Mouse Knight—perfectly mirroring the rusted statue from Room 1. The walls are covered in faded, cracked murals depicting the mouse striking down a shadowy, serpentine figure, followed by the mouse intentionally flooding the valley to seal the ruins.

The ancient knight clutches against its chest a masterwork sword in each hand. One bears a gleaming brass hilt with an egg-sized ruby in its pommel. The other is elegant silver with an obsidian black handle and a small diamond at the base. Both swords are broken roughly a foot from the hilt, their tips nestled among the Mouse Knight's robes. The breaks are clean, and the swords are otherwise perfectly preserved. Both pommels are molded in the shape of snake heads that clutch the gems in gaping fangs.
### The Exit
Behind the dais, thick birch roots have burst through the masonry ceiling over the centuries. Light filters down through the tangled roots, providing a natural, easy yet monotonous climb up a shaft that emerges safely inside a hollowed-out birch stump on the forest surface, miles away from the main entrance.
